# **How to Increase Average Order Value on Shopify in 2026**

More traffic is great as it means more people visiting your website, but it won't guarantee growth if it's low quality. You may end up with lots of visitors but very few conversions if the visitors aren't genuinely interested or qualified. As ad spend increases, Shopify stores require more profit from the sales they already make. In fact, getting more from already engaged customers is a strategy that can work.

That's where the importance of average order value lies. You can increase customer purchases by better recommendations, cross-selling strategies, special promotions related to buying experience and so on. You also need to ensure good customer experience to get the most out of each sale. In this way, a major portion of your increased sales can come from your existing customer base rather than increasing marketing spends.

This guide explains how to **increase average order value on Shopify** using practical strategies for 2026.

## **What Is the Average Order Value on Shopify?**

Average order value, or AOV, is the average amount a customer spends in one order.

**Average Order Value = Total Revenue ÷ Total Number of Orders**

So if your store gets 200 orders that amount to \$10,000 in sales, your AOV will be \$50.

AOV demonstrates how much revenue you make from a single completed transaction. Still, there are other key metrics to look at like profit margin, product cost discounts shipping, and returns. It is only profitable if customers buy more at a better margin. Shopify also uses AOV which is calculated as revenue divided by orders and it also suggests that looking at average AOV alone will not give you a clear picture of your success.

## **Why Is My Shopify Average Order Value Low?**

A low average order value usually implies customers are only buying the main product and completing the purchase without ever checking out other options.

Here are some typical reasons:

- The related products are not displayed at the right moment.
- Packages/bundles do not catch attention.
- Purchased product, total cost, quantity, and checkout button is all the cart displays.
- Gift targets and free shipping are not properly revealed.
- Sales of additional products are either very expensive or not related at all.
- Promotion of the mobile store isn't that clear in understanding the offer.

Trace the full path from the product page to cart. You want nothing less than the customers' spending being forced up. Just let your buyers find it more comfortable adding the helpful stuff without much effort.

## **How Can I Increase Average Order Value on Shopify Without More Ad Spend?**

It is better to begin with potential customers who have already included an item to their shopping basket because they have demonstrated buyer intent. That's why a well-suited suggestion will probably be much more relevant than another general advertisement.

An efficient and reliable Average Order Value (AOV) system should consist of a related item, a packaged deal bundle, a purchase goal, a simple discount offer, a convenient checkout, etc. besides the essential checkout step.

Shopify, in its suggestion, highlights the need to create bundles for customers to buy more of the same items, to upsell higher-priced items, to cross-sell related items, to offer a free shipping minimum, to use AI-based personalized recommendations, and to suggest the next steps to buyers after a sale to increase the order value.

Do not use all the strategies at the same time. You should test with one or two first by implementing them, evaluating the effectiveness of these tactics, and only then adding more tactics.

## **What Are the Best Shopify Upsell and Cross-Sell Strategies?**

A strategy of upselling is to convince the customer to take a more expensive version or a superior version of the same item. It is usually presented right after the purchase of the original product.

A cross-sell suggests an alternative that is not a competitor but complements the one you bought.

**For example:**

Face wash → larger size

Face wash → moisturizer

Protein powder → shaker

Laptop → protective sleeve

Dress → matching accessory

The recommendations should be genuine and not just sales pitches. Usually, customers are more inclined to buy a relatively low-cost supplementary item than another expensive item.

These offer combinations are best placed on the product details page or in the shopping cart before the final checkout or even after purchase. The additional items can be useful if one understands the need and the reason behind it.

## **How Do Product Bundles Increase AOV on Shopify?**

Bundles are an effective way for customers to purchase all the parts of a product they would need for use in one place rather than having to shop around in multiple places.

Types of product bundles that may prove beneficial:

- Starter packs
- Complete routine bundles
- Multipacks
- Mix-and-match bundles
- Frequently bought together sets
- Build-your-own combos

A skincare store could include a combination of cleansers, serums, moisturizers for sale as different levels of bundles. A food brand can design a morning bundle. An apparel or clothing retail store should put on a total wear set or an outfit set.

Use order data to look out for goods customers already purchase together. Display the overall sum of individual products, bundle price, the saving, and purpose of the set.

Baskets or bundles of products must not only offer a good bargain but they should also make it very much more convenient for customers. As Shopify, when one wants to create the bundle of products it is best to use what comes next: complementary products, buying data, showing clear savings, and margin-based pricing.

## **Do Frequently Bought Together Offers Work on Shopify?**

Best results in cross-merchandising are achieved when the offered products have a distinct relation, a logical link. A buyer of a coffeemaker will usually need coffee filters.

A customer buying shoes should have socks as an option. One looking to buy a shampoo will probably want a conditioner too.

Limit the choice. Two or three top recommendations should be presented rather than the full range, otherwise there's a risk that users will get lost in details, In particular if they have to make several buying decisions in one go

Also, every recommendation has to comprise:

- Product image
- Product name
- Price
- Benefit
- Add-cart button

Displaying the same products for recommendations to every shopper is not really helpful. Try, for instance, to show those matched to the item being checked, the item already put into a cart, the amount of items in the cart or past purchasing pattern.

## **What Is the Best Free Shipping Threshold for Shopify?**

Providing an example with zero payment is the simplest way of giving buyers a motivation to get at least an extra piece of product.

Consider your current average order value and most common purchase value to get an idea about the target you can go for. If many regularly spend \$40, \$50 or \$55 may look like realistic goals. Though, the \$100 target will appear too far away.

You can use a cart progress message displaying the target like:

- "You have just \$12 left for free shipping"
- "Get a free gift by adding one more item"
- "Spend another \$8 to get 10% off"

Apart from that, Shopify strongly recommends that incentives should be offered to common order values and also that a target placed too far will only have a negative effect on customer behavior.

## **How Can a Smart Cart Drawer Increase Shopify AOV?**

Shopping cart functionality shouldn't end with product display and checkout button. Cart becomes a useful order check and a recommendation source for products that the customer is planning to buy, all done without abandoning the cart.

**To make shopping cart drawer effective:**

- Relevant product upsells
- Suggestions of frequently bought together items
- Progress towards a free shipping or a free gift
- Savings from bundle options
- Promotion or coupon codes entry or availability of promotions
- Detailed subtotal
- Straightforward quantity increment/decrement
- Checkout button always accessible

Lay out the cart page carefully so customers are not bombarded with too many pop-ups, messages, countdowns, or offers, which might cause mistrust.

Having a mobile friendly shopping experience is very important. Price buttons progress and savings, and other messages should not be a problem for customers even if they are viewed on a smaller screen.

## **How Can AI Product Recommendations Increase AOV?**

AI product recommendations combine product relationship, customer orders, cart items, and customer behavior data to predict and recommend the most appropriate products.

This makes it possible for a shop to not just rely on 'top-selling' recommendation methods anymore. Rather a buyer can get suggestions of products that are relevant to the product they are considering or purchasing.

Some areas an AI can help with:

- From one product recommendation to another
- Products often bought together
- Packaging Ideas
- Upsells on carts tailored to your customers
- Tailored customer discounts
- Superior product pairings

Ai-powered product recommendations are among the AOV strategies that are already active in Shopify.

Good merchandising should be what AI supports and not replace it. Check recommendations periodically and eliminate combinations that are misleading, low-margin, out of stock or irrelevant.

## **Should I Use Discounts to Increase Average Order Value?**

While discounts are known to help customers spend more at checkout, they can also result in lower profits.

The way that you offer discounts is also important so that you don't lose sight of the main goal of generating a profit. One alternative is to tie discounts not to every product but with a certain level of spending or purchase.

- Two for one at 10% off
- Three for one at 15% off
- \$75 purchase to get a free product
- Include a recommended item at a special price
- Purchase reaches a certain value before unlocking a coupon

Check your final margin after you have factored in product costs, shipping payment fees and the discount.

When choosing your promotional options, you must find a balance whereby the customer perceives the offer as providing real value and the business, Then again, still sees a profit from the order.

## **How Do I Measure Whether My Shopify AOV Strategy Is Working?**

Avg. Order value is more than overall figure. Break down and track each offer to gain insight on their effectiveness.

Key metrics to track:

- Average order value
- Conversion rate
- Revenue per visitor
- Upsell clicks
- Upsell acceptance rate
- Bundle sales
- Coupon usage
- Checkout clicks
- Bundled revenue in cart
- Gross profit/order
- Returns/refunds

See if changes in one area lead to positive/negative change in related areas. Compare performance across products, devices, referral sources and customer segments.

AOV increase through different channels (web, email etc.) should not cause massive drop in conversion rate. The ideal marketing strategy is one that raises the order size and, at the same time, simplifies the purchasing experience for customers.

## **What Is the Fastest Way to Increase Average Order Value on Shopify?**

The starting point with the highest potential speed is usually the shopping cart.

- Determine your average AOV and common order value.
- Choose one lower-cost item that complements your top seller naturally.
- Use it as an upsell in cart or offer it as a frequently purchased together product.
- Make the free shipping or gift target reasonably attainable.
- Design only one useful product combination.
- Familiarize yourself with clicks, extra revenue, conversion rate, and profitability.
- Refine the proposal based on actual customer behavior.

## **Conclusion**

Motivating customers through more persuasive promotional activities will not help in raising average order values at Shopify stores in 2026. Rather, one should facilitate customers to know products, product combinations, and rewards that suit their needs at the right point of time.

Purposeful interplay between product page cart offers, and recommendations result in better possibility at each checkout of delivering more value to both the customer and the store.
